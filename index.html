AI Usage Badges

Why use these? They give readers a quick, honest heads-up about how much AI helped. That means fewer surprises for collaborators, clearer trust with customers, and a simple way to align team policy (no policy docs required). Pick one badge, show it near your work, and people instantly know the level of AI involvement.

Concept

Process, Not Credit

Each badge states the level and order of human/AI contribution used to produce the artifact.

One Badge per Artifact

Pick the badge that best matches the artifact. If multiple steps occurred, choose the highest AI involvement that applies.

Honesty First

Badge selection is an honesty signal for readers, reviewers, and downstream users.

Usage Guidelines

  • Use one badge per artifact: emails, slide decks, briefs, FAQs, customer updates.
  • Pick the highest AI involvement that happened; if unsure, choose Human–AI Co-Created.
  • Put it where people look: email signature/footer, slide title or footer, document cover.
  • Keep the badge with the file so it travels (PDF footer, shared drive folder, or doc header).
  • Add alt text for accessibility and in case images are blocked in email.
  • Use the SVG for web/docs; use PNG 1x/2x in email and slideware for maximum compatibility.

Badges

Human Original

Created without AI assistance.

Human Original • AI Polished

Human wrote it AI only refinished (grammar tone tightening).

Human Written • AI Reviewed

Human authored AI used for review or quality checks only.

AI Suggested • Human Approved

AI proposed options human selected and lightly adjusted.

Human Curated

Human selected or arranged AI outputs from multiple candidates.

Human–AI Co-Created

Human and AI collaborated iteratively shared authorship of ideas and wording.

AI Drafted • Human Edited

AI first draft then substantive human rewriting or fact-checking.

AI Drafted

AI produced the first draft; minimal human edits.

AI Generated

AI produced the final artifact with no meaningful human edits.
